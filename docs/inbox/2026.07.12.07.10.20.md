Yes. Right now “research” is mostly a folder plus long prose, not a defined act with inputs, method, evidence, outputs, and acceptance criteria. That means a document can look serious without establishing what was actually investigated, which sources support which claims, what uncertainty remains, or how the result is allowed to inform a decision.

A research artifact should be treated as **evidence with provenance**, not simply a written report. FAIR’s reuse principle explicitly includes clear provenance and relevant community standards, while ACM artifact evaluation distinguishes documented, complete, exercisable artifacts from merely claimed results. [acm](https://www.acm.org/publications/policies/artifact-review-and-badging-current)

## Define research activity

A **research activity** is a bounded, reproducible attempt to reduce uncertainty about a named question through a declared method and recorded evidence.

It is not:

- Reading material without recording why it was selected.
- Producing a broad “overview” without a decision/question it informs.
- Collecting links or generated summaries.
- Writing conclusions whose source-level support cannot be inspected.
- Calling a web search result a finding.

A research activity exists only when it has these six elements:

| Element | Required question |
|---|---|
| **Question** | What uncertainty is being reduced? |
| **Scope** | What is in/out, including time window, domain, and source types? |
| **Method** | How were sources found, selected, evaluated, and synthesized? |
| **Evidence set** | What exact primary/secondary materials were inspected? |
| **Claims** | What does each finding assert, with confidence and support? |
| **Disposition** | What decision, design, task, or open question does it inform? |

If any are absent, call it a **note**, **inbox item**, **reading log**, or **hypothesis**—not research.

## Define research artifacts

A research artifact is any durable, addressable output necessary to inspect, repeat, challenge, reuse, or correctly interpret a research activity.

For Epiphany, I would use an explicit artifact taxonomy:

| Artifact kind | Meaning | Minimum content |
|---|---|---|
| `research-brief` | A bounded plan before investigation | Question, decision target, scope, method, exit criteria |
| `source-record` | Provenance record for one external or internal source | Identifier/URL/path, version/date accessed, source type, authority, license/access, extract references |
| `source-extract` | Exact or bounded paraphrased evidence from a source | Source-record link, locator, extraction method, excerpt/hash where permitted |
| `finding` | A narrow supported statement | Claim, supporting and contradicting evidence, confidence, limitations |
| `synthesis` | A structured comparison across findings | Question, method summary, agreements, conflicts, uncertainty, implications |
| `research-report` | Human-readable review package | Links to brief, source set, findings, synthesis, implications |
| `research-gap` | A recorded unanswered or contested question | What is unknown, why evidence is insufficient, next method |
| `replication-record` | Evidence that a result/process was rerun | Inputs, environment, commands, result, divergence |
| `decision-input` | The subset of findings offered to a design/ADR | Findings used, not-used evidence, recommendation, decision limits |

The report is therefore a **view over a research ledger**, not the lone source of truth.

## Research evidence ladder

Research needs the same epistemic tiers as the product:

```text
source observed
  -> extract observed
    -> finding derived from extracts
      -> synthesis / interpretation
        -> decision input
          -> accepted ADR or design decision
```

Each promotion must preserve the preceding evidence. In particular:

- A source is not a finding.
- A finding is not a decision.
- A synthesis is not consensus.
- A cited report is not proof that its claims apply to Epiphany.
- An LLM-generated summary is a **derived aid**, never the sole evidence for a durable technical claim.

This gives a clean answer to “what counts as research?”: research is the process that produces a traceable chain from question to sources to extracts to bounded findings to a stated decision implication.

## Source and claim records

Keep source/claim metadata machine-readable, ideally EDN because the project is Clojure-first:

```clojure
{:source/id #uuid "..."
 :source/kind :official-documentation
 :source/title "..."
 :source/locator "https://..."
 :source/published-at "2025-..."
 :source/accessed-at "2026-07-12T..."
 :source/version "..."
 :source/authority :primary
 :source/license :unknown
 :source/content-digest "sha256:..."
 :source/status :observed}
```

```clojure
{:finding/id #uuid "..."
 :finding/claim
 "Adapter contract tests are needed because port-shape validation alone does not validate function behavior."
 :finding/status :derived
 :finding/confidence :high
 :finding/supports [#uuid "..."]
 :finding/contradicts []
 :finding/limitations
 ["Applies to the defined observation-port contract, not every future adapter."]
```

This reflects FAIR’s emphasis on identifiers, rich metadata, formal shared representation, qualified references, and provenance as conditions for reuse. [nlm.nih](https://www.nlm.nih.gov/oet/ed/cde/tutorial/02-200.html)

## Research quality gates

A document checker can enforce the structural part:

**Error:**

- A research report has no question, method, scope, source set, finding set, or limitations section.
- A report claims to support an ADR/design but has no `decision-target`.
- A finding has no source/extract link.
- A source URL/path is malformed or unavailable at recording time, unless explicitly captured as unavailable.
- A report marked `accepted` has no conflict/limitations assessment.
- A research-derived design claim has no traceable finding reference.

**Warning:**

- All sources are one authority type or one vendor.
- No primary source is used where one should exist.
- No contrary evidence / no search for disagreement is recorded.
- The conclusion is stronger than its declared confidence.
- “Best,” “proven,” “always,” “never,” or “solves” appears without a scoped claim and evidence link.
- The source set has only search-result snippets rather than inspected sources.
- A report has no stated stop condition or is too broad for its declared question.

**Human review:**

- Whether selection bias is acceptable.
- Whether the method was sufficient for the decision’s consequence.
- Whether sources genuinely support the interpretation.
- Whether a claimed absence of evidence is justified.
- Whether decision implications overreach.

## Research maturity levels

Avoid making every investigation meet the burden of a systematic literature review. Instead, declare its tier.

| Tier | Use case | Required evidence |
|---|---|---|
| `orientation` | Learn vocabulary / map a new area | Question, limited sources, notes, explicit non-decision status |
| `decision-support` | Inform a design choice | Brief, declared method, source records, findings, alternatives/conflicts, limitations |
| `implementation-grounding` | Support a specific engineering change | Decision-support evidence plus direct documentation/spec/API verification and a runnable spike where appropriate |
| `reproduction` | Verify a published or internal result | Inputs, environment, commands, exact result/divergence, evidence artifact |
| `evaluation` | Compare alternatives | Protocol, criteria, datasets/fixtures, measurements, analysis, threats to validity |

A research report should state its tier at the top. It is invalid to use `orientation` research as the sole justification for an irreversible architectural decision.

## Research is an activity record

The act itself should be represented, not reconstructed from polished prose:

```clojure
{:research-activity/id #uuid "..."
 :research/question "Which Clojure mutation-testing workflow can test validation removal under deps.edn?"
 :research/tier :implementation-grounding
 :research/started-at ...
 :research/completed-at ...
 :research/method
 {:queries ["Clojure mutation testing Clojure CLI"
            "Heretic mutation testing Clojure"
            "Mutant Clojure Leiningen"]
  :selection {:must-support [:deps-edn :targeted-namespaces :reproducible-report]}
  :evaluation [:installation :test-run :deliberate-validation-removal]}
 :research/inputs [...]
 :research/outputs [...]
 :research/status :completed}
```

This prevents retrospective fabrication of a method after a conclusion is already chosen.

## Documentation work sequence

I would not write templates for every artifact kind immediately. Begin with the two most dangerous gaps:

1. **Research brief** — prevents unbounded, ungrounded investigations.
2. **Research report plus source/finding records** — prevents prose from masquerading as evidence.
3. **Design template** — requires explicit grounding links and separates findings from decisions.
4. **Engineering-story template** — requires concrete contracts, tests, and completion evidence.
5. **ADR and process templates** — once their upstream inputs are normalized.
6. **Examples, anti-examples, and checker rules** for each, one kind at a time.

The research template should be dogfooded by researching the documentation-governance process itself: sources on ADR practice, software documentation quality, research provenance, artifact evaluation, and Markdown lint/document-schema tooling. ACM’s artifact criteria offer a useful model: an artifact must be documented, consistent, complete, exercisable, and provide verification/validation evidence; separate badges distinguish functionality from independently reproduced results. [sysartifacts.github](https://sysartifacts.github.io/eurosys2024/badges)

## A governing rule

I would write this into the future documentation/process standard:

> A research artifact may reduce uncertainty. It may not silently create a decision, establish a fact beyond its source evidence, or substitute an unsupported synthesis for a traceable finding.

That aligns research governance with Epiphany’s central rule: never silently turn similarity into identity. Here the equivalent is: **never silently turn reading into knowledge, or a summary into evidence.**
